Ottawa covid-19 wastewater surveillance (development page) Open science is awesome

The National Capital Region is one of the first communities in Canada to conduct daily wastewater readings to help inform its community response in the fight against covid-19, thanks to innovative research from the CHEO Research Institute (CHEO RI) and the University of Ottawa (uOttawa).

Learn more about wastewater epidemiology and its role in covid-19 surveillance on Ottawa Public Healthâ€™s website.

The science supporting wastewater surveillance is rapidly evolving. The plots are currently for research only and presented to the public for discussion. Wastewater surveillance is currently viewed as having several uses, including:

  • Early identification of new cases of covid-19 when there is a low level of covid-19 infection.
  • A population perspective of covid-19 infection that is not influenced by the level of covid-19 testing. Everyone pees and poos, including people with and without covid-19. People with covid-19 shed SARS-CoV-2 in their poo, regardless of whether they receive a covid-19 test or diagnosis.

However, at this time, we recommend caution when interpretation daily and short-term variation in the viral signal. Covid-19 wastewater signal is helpful when interpreted alongside other covid-19 surveillance measures, taking into considerations of the strengths and limitations of each measure. The accurately test SARS-CoV-2 is improving, as scientist understand the role of factors such differences in sewage systems and laboratory protocols.

7-day averages are rolling averages, calculated using the results for the previous days. The frequency of testing changes over time. Currently, Ottawa wastewater testing is performed 5 days each week.

* New cases (reported date) is the date reported by the laboratory. New cases (episode date) is the earliest onset, test or reported date.

* Viral copies data is normalized using a seasonally stable fecal biomarker; pepper mild mottle virus (PMMoV). See methods for more details.

^ Percent change in 7-day average is calculated by comparing the 7-day average (previous day 1 to 7) with a lagged 5-day average (days 8 to 14).
